5) Is the Holy Spirit Just the Power of God or a Divine Person?

Some believe that the Holy Spirit is merely the power of God and not a divine person. However, the Bible reveals more:

  • The Hebrew word for "Spirit" (ruach) and the Greek word (pneuma) can mean breath, wind, mind, or life. However, many passages refer to the Spirit as more than just an impersonal force.
  • Even though pneuma is neuter in Greek, it is often referred to using the masculine pronoun "He" rather than "it" (John 16:3, 7, 8, 13).
  • The Holy Spirit performs actions that only a person with intelligence, will, and individuality can do, such as convicting, teaching, guiding, and glorifying Jesus (John 16:7-14).
  • Paul states that the Spirit distributes gifts "as He wills" (1 Corinthians 12:11), showing He has a personal will.

üìñ Matthew 28:19 - "Baptizing them in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit."

Would it not be illogical for Jesus to say, "in the name of the Father, the Son, and the power of the Father"? Jesus clearly distinguished the Holy Spirit as a divine person, not merely an impersonal force.

üìñ Matthew 12:31-32 - "Every sin and blasphemy will be forgiven, but the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven. Anyone who speaks a word against the Son of Man will be forgiven, but anyone who speaks against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven, either in this age or in the age to come."

If the Holy Spirit were merely an impersonal energy, why would Jesus issue such a severe warning? The truth is that the Holy Spirit is a divine person, actively working in our conversion and spiritual growth.

Minimizing His importance risks endangering our very Christian identity and salvation.
