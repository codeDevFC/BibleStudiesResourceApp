1.4 Bible Studies: Jesus is the Son of God

And Our Savior

Purpose:

To understand why Jesus Christ stands at the center of our faith & Christian experience. In a future study, we will delve deeper into the divine nature of Jesus.

Introduction

We call ourselves Christians because we recognize Jesus Christ as the foundation of our faith. But who is Jesus?

Throughout history, people have had different views of Him: a great man, a philanthropist, a moral teacher, a religious leader, or even a hero of faith. Some believe He is more than a man and call Him the Son of God.

1) What Did Peter Say About Jesus?

üìñ Matthew 16:16 - "Simon Peter answered, 'You are the Christ, the Son of the living God.'"

Peter saw Jesus as much more than a prophet; he recognized Him as the Son of the living God.

2) What Does It Mean That Jesus Is the Son of God?

Unlike others who saw Jesus only as a man, Peter acknowledged His divine essence. Jesus was not merely a creature of God; He was the Son of God, uniquely connected to the Father in a way that no one else was.

3) What Does It Mean That Jesus Is the Christ?

The term Christ comes from the Greek Christos, which translates the Hebrew Messiah, meaning the Anointed One.

In Israel, kings, priests, and prophets were anointed with oil as a sign of divine authority. Jesus, however, was the ultimate Anointed One, fulfilling all these roles:

  • üìñ A Prophet - Foretold in Deuteronomy 18:15-16

    • Jesus Christ embodied all the ministries of salvation that God had entrusted to His anointed ones throughout history. He was destined to bring freedom and salvation to His people. In other words, He would be their Savior.

üìñ 1 Peter 1:17-21 - "You were redeemed not with perishable things like silver or gold, but with the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish or defect."

Conclusion

Understanding Jesus as the Son of God and our Savior is central to our faith. His life, death, and resurrection reveal His divine nature and the depth of God's love for us.
