1.6 - God Speaks to Us

Through the Bible

Purpose: To understand the Bible as the foundation of the Christian faith.

The Christian faith is not a philosophy; it does not originate from human intellectual inquiry. It does not come from man but from God.

If it came from man, what advantage would we have? By following it, we would only be following our own imaginations, yet we would remain alone in the universe. How can we know that what we believe truly comes from God? Only if God Himself tells us! Does such a "Word of God" exist? Yes, we believe that it is the Bible.

1) What is the Bible physically?

The word "Bible" comes from the Greek Biblia, which means "books." It is divided into two parts:

Old Testament (O.T.)

39 books written before Christ's incarnation

New Testament (N.T.)

27 books and letters written after Jesus' death

Total: 66 writings

3) Why are the apostles and prophets so important?

Because of sin, humanity lost direct communion with God and can no longer hear His voice directly. The apostles and prophets were chosen by God as His spokespeople to communicate His will to us.

Prophet

"One who speaks on behalf of..."

Apostle

"One who is sent"

The difference between these two is that prophets received their messages through visions and dreams (Numbers 12:6), while apostles primarily received theirs through Jesus' direct teachings (1 John 1:1-4).

Moses was an exception, as God spoke to him "face to face," directly (Numbers 12:7-8).

8) Is the Bible too difficult for ordinary people?

Historical Misconception:

In Catholicism, the Bible was not widely accessible, leading some to think it should be left to scholars.

Luke 10:21

Jesus thanks God for revealing truth to the humble, not just the wise.

The Bible was written for ordinary people.

  • Most passages are accessible to all
  • Even children can understand its core messages
  • Some passages may require more study
