Who is Jesus?

Purpose:

To understand Jesus' human-divine nature in order to better comprehend the value and scope of His love, and to increase our trust in Him.

Introduction

We are Christians because Christ is at the center of our faith and hope. Therefore, we should know Him very well. But who is Jesus? There is something extraordinary about His nature. His own disciples, one day, seeing the manifestation of His power, exclaimed: "Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey Him?" (Mark 4:41). Another time, when Jesus asked what His disciples thought He was, Peter answered: "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" (Matthew 16:16). From Jesus' reply ("Flesh and blood has not revealed this to you, but My Father who is in heaven"), we can understand how comprehending Jesus' true nature lies beyond human rationality. However, God Himself, in the Bible, has revealed something to help us understand that in Jesus there is much more than simply a holy man. Let us consider some texts.

1) Jesus is not a creature of God

  • He is the Creator (Colossians 1:13-16)
  • He existed before creation (Colossians 1:17)
  • When God created, at the beginning of all things, He already existed (John 1:2,3)

2) Jesus is God

  • Isaiah calls the future Messiah God (Isaiah 9:6)
  • Jesus is God together with the Father (John 1:1)
  • Jesus is in the Father's bosom, intimately united with Him, comes from Him, not from outside Him like creatures (John 1:18)
  • Jesus is God who deserves to be praised for eternity (Romans 9:5; Hebrews 1:1-3)
  • Jesus defines Himself as Yahweh, the only existing God (John 8:58 with Exodus 3:14)

3) What name was to be given to Jesus?

Matthew 1:22

Jesus is Emmanuel – God with us.

4) Jesus is the Angel of Yahweh - God

The Old Testament presents a mysterious being called "the angel of Yahweh." This is not just an angel but THE Angel of Yahweh, as if something unique. His relationship with Yahweh (God's name in the O.T.) is so close that He is sometimes identified as Yahweh Himself.

Biblical Evidence:

  • Genesis 18:1,2,16-33; 19:1 - Three persons appear to Abraham. Two go to seek Lot in Sodom while the third remains with Abraham and is identified as Yahweh.
  • Genesis 32:24-30 - God appears to Jacob in a mysterious way.
  • Exodus 3:1,2,4-14 - The Angel of Yahweh reveals Himself as Yahweh and asks Moses to remove his sandals in worship.
  • Joshua 5:13-15 - Jesus appears to Joshua as the commander of God's army.
  • Judges 6:11-14,16,12-23 - The angel of Yahweh appears to Gideon.
  • Judges 13:3,15-23 - The Angel of Yahweh appears to Samson's parents.
  • Malachi 3:1 - Prophecy concerning John the Baptist and the Lord's coming.

5) Being God, what did Jesus accept to become?

John 1:14; Philippians 2:5-9; Hebrews 2:14

Jesus, the eternal Son of God, accepted becoming a poor human being. He humbled Himself by becoming what we are, to manifest His love and save us.

6) Does Jesus' incarnation eliminate His divinity?

Colossians 2:9

Not at all. In Jesus continues to dwell bodily all the fullness of the Deity.

7) How did Thomas call Jesus after the resurrection?

John 20:28

By calling Jesus "My Lord and my God," Thomas wasn't expressing surprise at Jesus' resurrection, as some erroneously think. A Jew would never use God's name in such a way. Instead, he recognized Jesus' divinity and lordship, expressing what would become the faith of the entire Christian church.

8) Knowing who Jesus was and what He became for us, how can we react to His sacrifice?

1 Corinthians 2:1,2; Galatians 2:20

Can we make the Apostle Paul's conviction our own? To know nothing except Jesus Christ and Him crucified, and to live by faith in the Son of God who loved us and gave Himself for us.
