5.5 The Sabbath in the Old Testament

The Sabbath: A Divine Appointment

When we love someone, we desire to dedicate time to them because time is life, and therefore much more precious than any material gift. The same happens with God: He loves us and offers us special time as a sign of His love for us.

In This Study We Will:

  • Explore the biblical significance of the Sabbath
  • Understand its role in the Old Testament
  • Discover God's purpose in giving this special time

This is part one of a three-part series examining the Sabbath in Scripture.

1. The Importance of the Sabbath in the Decalogue

Exodus 20:8-11

Consider:

a. This is the only commandment that begins with "remember." Why do you think God did this?

b. This is the longest of the Ten Commandments. Do you think this fact also indicates its importance?

2. The Meaning of the Sabbath

Creator and Father

Exodus 20:11

Celebrates God as Creator of the world and as our Father

Sign of Grace

Exodus 31:13; Ezekiel 20:20

A sign of God's sanctifying grace toward us. Before being a sign of our love for God, it is a sign of His love for us. It reminds us that God created us because He loves us and chose us to be His special people.

3. How Should We Observe the Sabbath?

Rest from Work

Exodus 20:8,9

Not working or engaging in worldly activities

Dedication to God

Exodus 20:8

Dedicating this time to God, seeking Him and serving Him. "Sanctifying" means it's not just a time of inactivity but a special experience with God.

Community Worship

Leviticus 23:3

Meeting with God's family to worship Him as our common Father

4. Which Day Does God Ask Us to Sanctify?

It is the seventh day of the week. It commemorates that God created the world in six days and rested (ceased from activity) on the seventh.

Exodus 20:7

By resting on the same day, we celebrate His creation and rejoice in Him.

This day corresponds to our modern Saturday: the modern word "Sabbath" derives from the Hebrew "shabbat" meaning rest.

5. Can Sunday Be Observed as the Seventh Day?

No. Sunday is the first day of the week as every well-informed person knows.

Luke 23:54-24:2

Friday

Crucifixion day (Preparation Day)

Saturday

Sabbath (Seventh Day)

Sunday

Resurrection (First Day)

6. How Do We Know Our Modern Saturday Corresponds to God's Original Sabbath?

Historical Evidence

Exodus 16:22-24

The manna miracle in the wilderness:

  • Double portion gathered on sixth day
  • No spoilage on Sabbath
  • No manna on seventh day

Continuous Observance

  • Jewish historical observance
  • Jesus' participation (Luke 4:15)
  • Known calendar changes
  • Agreement between Jewish and Christian calendars

7. When Was the Sabbath Given to Humanity?

Genesis 2:1-3

At Creation

Divine Blessing

Contains special blessing for observers

Sanctification

Set apart for sacred use by humanity

8. Was the Sabbath Given Only to the Jewish People?

Genesis 2:1-3

When God created the world and gave the Sabbath, the Jewish people didn't exist: only Adam and Eve, progenitors of all humanity.

Deuteronomy 4:6; Isaiah 43:10

God intended His law to reach all peoples through Israel

Isaiah 56:6-7

Foreigners who observe the Sabbath will be welcomed into God's people

9. When Does the Sabbath Begin?

Leviticus 23:32

Biblical Time Reckoning

  • Evening to evening pattern
  • Based on natural cycles (Genesis 1:16)
  • Begins at sunset Friday
Luke 23:54-56

Example from Christ's burial: Activities ceased at sunset before Sabbath

10. What Should Be Our Attitude Toward This Commandment?

Love-Based Obedience

John 14:15,21

We desire to obey God because we love Him

Complete Commitment

James 2:10

We aim to obey all commandments as breaking one breaks the whole law

Joyful Observance

Isaiah 58:13-14

We observe the Sabbath with joy as it testifies of God as our caring Father

Trust in Blessing

Genesis 2:3

We trust in God's blessing placed on this day

Additional In-depth Studies

In our study, we've seen that God's commandment teaches that the Sabbath is not a day for work but for God. Work isn't just paid employment but includes any activity that:

  • Corresponds to material interests
  • Distracts from spiritual values
  • Interferes with the sanctification characteristic of the rest day
Nehemiah 13:15-22

This passage specifically includes:

  • Commercial activities
  • Buying and selling
  • Loading merchandise
Isaiah 58:13

Uses the comprehensive phrase "doing your own business," which can include:

  • House maintenance (e.g., painting)
  • Regular household chores (e.g., laundry)
  • Personal business matters

Important Understanding

"Not working" doesn't mean total inactivity. The following study will show how this time should be used for:

  • Spiritual growth and development
  • Service to others
  • Acts of mercy and necessity
  • Ministry to those in need
Isaiah 66:22,23

"'As the new heavens and the new earth that I make will endure before me,' declares the LORD, 'so will your name and descendants endure. From one New Moon to another and from one Sabbath to another, all mankind will come and bow down before me,' says the LORD."

Key Considerations

  • Association of Sabbath with new moons
  • Messianic context
  • Symbolic representation
  • Eternal worship perspective

Scholar's Perspective

"The text doesn't speak so much about the observance of these two days in themselves; instead it emphasizes the continuity of worship, a characteristic of the new earth... The author uses 'from...to' to express the eternal nature of worship."

- Prof. Jacques B. Doukhan

Understanding in Context

  • The prophet speaks from his Jewish perspective
  • Uses familiar worship patterns to describe future reality
  • Emphasizes continuity of worship rather than specific days
  • Points to eternal relationship with God
