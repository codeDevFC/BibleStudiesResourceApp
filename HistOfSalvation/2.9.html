Our Heavenly Experience (The Millennium)

Purpose:

To understand what happens after the Lord's return, eliminating false millennial expectations (regarding Christ's millennial kingdom) and understanding how the millennium expresses God's love and respect for His creatures' freedom.

So far, we've seen that when Jesus returns, all believers will go to heaven with Him. But what will happen on earth? What will the saved do in heaven? Will they stay there for eternity? We'll find answers to these questions by studying what the Bible says in Revelation 20 about a special period we call the "millennium".

1) What will the saved do during the millennium?

  • Reign with Jesus

    Revelation 20:4

  • Judge the Wicked

    Revelation 20:4; 1 Corinthians 6:2-3; Matthew 19:28

    In ancient times, the king held all main powers of the State, including judgment (1 Kings 3:7; Jeremiah 22:15-16). Those who sit on thrones to judge are the same who reign with Jesus.

2) What is the significance of this judgment?

While the Bible doesn't explicitly explain this, we can understand that:

  • God wants to help us understand His reasons for not being able to save the lost
  • God shares His thoughts with His children (John 15:15; Amos 3:7)
  • By asking us to judge the wicked, it's as if God asks us to judge His own justice
Revelation 19:1-2; 16:7

"Hallelujah! Salvation and glory and power belong to our God, for true and just are His judgments."

3) When does the millennium begin?

At Jesus' second coming. We can understand this from:

  1. Context of Revelation 19:11 onwards
  2. Saints judge when God's Kingdom is established (Matthew 19:28)
  3. Saints' resurrection occurs at Jesus' return (1 Thessalonians 4:13-18)

4) What happens at the beginning of the millennium?

Dead Saints

Rise gloriously and go to heaven

1 Thessalonians 4:16-17

Living Saints

Are glorified and go to heaven

1 Thessalonians 4:17; 1 Corinthians 15:52

Dead Wicked

Remain in their graves

Revelation 20:5

Living Wicked

Die

Revelation 19:17-18,21

5) What will the earth be like during the millennium?

With all saints in heaven and all wicked dead, the earth will be devoid of human life. Only Satan will remain, bound by a chain of circumstances. He remains with his angels on an earth that has returned to being an abyss (Revelation 20:3), the same word found in the Greek translation of Genesis 1:2 describing the earth as "without form and void" before God created life.

Yet from this desolation, God will again bring forth a world full of life at the millennium's end.

6) What happens at the end of the millennium?

  1. The Wicked Resurrect

    Revelation 20:5

  2. Satan Unbound

    No longer alone, his "chains" are loosed and he can again tempt humans (Revelation 20:7-8)

  3. New Jerusalem Descends

    With all saints inside (Revelation 21:2)

  4. Final Destruction

    The wicked, led by Satan, attempt to capture the holy city but are destroyed (Revelation 20:9). This is the second death, which is eternal (Revelation 20:6,14)

7) Why does God bring the wicked back to life at the millennium's end if they die again?

The Bible doesn't directly answer this question, but we can understand possible reasons:

Justice Demonstration

To provide conscious punishment for their sins

Final Vindication

Their final rebellion, when they have no excuse for unbelief, demonstrates that God truly couldn't save them because they had no desire to live by His principles of love and justice

8) How should we understand the final universal judgment described in Revelation 20:11-15?

This scene represents the moment when all humanity, good and evil, stands before God's throne, just after the wicked's resurrection and before their final punishment.

  • Saints inside New Jerusalem
  • Wicked surrounding it
  • All before their Creator
  • Each receiving eternal destiny confirmation
Romans 14:10-11

"For we will all stand before God's judgment seat... every knee will bow... every tongue will acknowledge God."

9) What happens after the millennium?

Revelation 21:3-4

"Now the dwelling of God is with men, and he will live with them. They will be his people, and God himself will be with them and be their God. He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain, for the old order of things has passed away."

Additional Insights

1. Are There Other Biblical Texts Describing the Millennium?

Revelation 20 is the only text explicitly mentioning the millennium. However, another text implies it:

Isaiah's Prophecy

Isaiah 24:17-23
Key Elements:
  • Earth "shaken" (v.19)
  • Sun "ashamed" (v.23)
  • Similar to Jesus' end-time signs (Matthew 24:29)

"In that day the LORD will punish the powers in the heavens above and the kings on the earth below... they will be herded together like prisoners bound in a dungeon; they will be shut up in prison and be punished after many days."

2. Do All Christians Interpret the Millennium the Same Way?

Historical Views

  • Early Church Period:

    Some viewed it as the church age before Jesus' return

  • Augustine's View:

    Interpreted as first millennium of church history

Modern Interpretation Issues

Common Misinterpretation:

Some believe the Millennium will be an era of prosperity on earth before Jesus' return, when:

  • Satan is bound
  • Nations convert
  • Earth becomes paradise-like
Why This View Is Problematic:
  1. Contradicts Revelation 20-21's timing
  2. Ignores absence of living beings on earth
  3. Overlooks that post-millennium only has death sentences
  4. Creates moral issues regarding Satan's release

The Moral Problem of Satan's Release

Critical Moral Question:

If without Satan the whole world becomes good, why would God release him to cause humans to fall into sin again?

The Theological Problem

This would suggest that God uses Satan as a tool to tempt nations, making Satan almost an instrument of God for the perdition of many people - a concept entirely foreign to biblical teaching.

Our Biblical Understanding

  • Satan's Release: Not God's specific will but a result of changing circumstances
  • Biblical Evidence:

    Revelation 20 deliberately avoids suggesting God wants Satan to tempt humans:

    • God explicitly binds Satan to prevent temptation (v. 4)
    • When unbound, Satan acts on his own initiative (vv. 4,7)
    • No divine intention mentioned in Satan's release

The Real Issue

The support for alternative interpretations stems not from exegesis of Revelation 20, but from the presumption that certain prophecies about Israel's kingdom restoration must be literally fulfilled during the millennium.

We believe the biblical millennium has no connection to literal Israel because Scripture explicitly links it to:

  • The Christian experience of salvation
  • The judgment of the world

And nothing more.

Biblical Understanding

The millennium has nothing to do with earthly Israel but relates explicitly to Christian salvation experience and world judgment.

Key Points to Remember

The Millennium's Purpose

  • Time for saints to understand God's judgments
  • Demonstration of God's justice and love
  • Period of earth's desolation

Key Events

  • Begins with Christ's second coming
  • Saints reign with Christ in heaven
  • Earth remains desolate
  • Ends with final judgment

Final Outcomes

  • God's justice fully vindicated
  • Sin's origin and consequences understood
  • Preparation for eternal kingdom
