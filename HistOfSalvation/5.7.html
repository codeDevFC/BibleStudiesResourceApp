5.7 Sunday in the Bible

Key Question

If Sabbath observance is clearly taught throughout the Bible, why do many Christians observe Sunday? Is there any biblical support for this practice?

1. Sunday References in Scripture

In the Bible, Sunday is mentioned only in the New Testament as "the first day of the week":

Resurrection Accounts

  • Matthew 28:1
  • Mark 16:2,9
  • Luke 24:1
  • John 20:1,19

These texts simply state the fact of resurrection, without designating it as a day of rest or worship.

Other References

  • Acts 20:7
  • 1 Corinthians 16:2

We will examine these texts in detail to understand their context.

2. John 20:19 - The Disciples' Meeting

John 20:19

Does this prove that Sunday had become a meeting day in honor of Jesus' resurrection?

Key Understanding:

Not at all. The text clarifies that:

  • The disciples weren't gathered to celebrate the resurrection - they didn't yet believe in it
  • They were "afraid of the Jews"
  • This was a meeting of confused people seeking comfort in common friendship
  • They gathered to discuss the dramatic events that had overwhelmed them

3. Acts 20:6-9 - The Meeting at Troas

Acts 20:6-9

"After the Days of Unleavened Bread... on the first day of the week, when we were gathered together to break bread, Paul talked with them, intending to depart on the next day..."

Unique Circumstances

  • Not a regular meeting
  • Night gathering
  • Special occasion due to Paul's departure

Time Calculation

  • Biblical days begin at sunset
  • Night portion came first
  • Meeting extended from Sabbath into first day

Context

  • Paul spent previous week with church
  • Extended Sabbath meeting due to departure
  • Not a regular Sunday worship service

Detailed Analysis of Key Points

1 Corinthians 16:1-4

"Now concerning the collection for the saints, as I directed the churches of Galatia, so you are to do as well. On the first day of every week, each of you is to put aside and save as he may prosper, so that no collections be made when I come..."

Key Observations:

  • No communal collection or church meeting mentioned
  • Individual action "at home"
  • Personal setting, not public worship
  • Collection to be gathered later by Paul's representatives

Why First Day?

Sabbath Practice

Following Pharisaic tradition, handling money was avoided on Sabbath

Practical Timing

After Sabbath reflection on God's blessings, decisions about offerings could be made

Revelation 1:9-10

"I, John... was in the Spirit on the Lord's day..."

Greek Term Analysis

"Kuriake hemera" (Lord's day/signorial day)

  • First certain reference to Sunday by this name appears much later
  • Different from Old Testament "Day of the Lord" terminology

Historical Context

  • Imperial usage: Emperor's birthday
  • Christians recognized only Jesus as Lord (1 Cor 8:6)
  • More likely refers to Sabbath, which Jesus claimed lordship over

Early Church Phase

  • Observed Sabbath
  • Protected under Jewish legal status
  • No conflict with Roman authority

Transition Phase

  • Jewish rebellions against Rome
  • Loss of Jewish legal protection
  • Christians distancing from Jewish practices
  • Gradual adoption of Sunday practices

Roman Influence

  • Sunday as day of sun worship
  • December 25th adoption for Christmas
  • Church of Rome's growing influence
  • Gradual replacement of Sabbath
Daniel 7:25

Prophecy of a power that would "intend to change times and law"

Prophetic Implications:

  • Predicted attempt to change God's law
  • Represents opposition to divine authority
  • Not divine authorization for change

Our Response

John 14:15,21

"If you love me, keep my commandments"

Our Response

John 14:15,21

We love Jesus and desire to obey His commandments

The prophesied change represents opposition to God's law, not divine authorization
