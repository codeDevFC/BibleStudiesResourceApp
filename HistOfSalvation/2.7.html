The Return of Jesus

Purpose:

To explain the meaning, importance, manner, and consequences of Jesus' return. To look confidently toward our future and be protected from false teachings.

Introduction

When we say that Jesus came to earth to save humanity, we cannot overlook the fact that suffering and death still exist. Through His first coming and death, Jesus laid the foundation for our forgiveness, transformed us into children of God, and began the work that will lead to our salvation. Salvation is something that already belongs to us spiritually speaking, but the fullness of its realization still lies ahead, both for us and for all creation which "groans and labors" (Romans 8:22). This is why He says that for now, "we were saved in hope" (Romans 8:24), and hope is something that drives us to wait with "patience" for something still future (Romans 8:25). This salvation will be fully realized when Jesus returns again to restore everything to its best reality according to God's plan of love.

Summary

As Christians, we believe that Jesus died for our sins, rose again, and ascended to heaven. But He has not abandoned us. Not only did He promise to be with us spiritually always (Matthew 28:20), but He promised to return to take us to be with Him eternally (John 14:1-4).

Key Aspects of Christ's Return

  • For Paul, this is the "blessed hope" (Titus 2:13)
  • Early Christians used it as their greeting "Maranatha" (1 Corinthians 16:21)
  • Without this promise, we would have no other hope
  • Only at Jesus' return will we enjoy the fullness of salvation
  • The dead in Christ will rise, and with the living, be transformed and taken with Jesus (Matthew 24:31; 1 Corinthians 15:51-55; 1 Thessalonians 4:13-18)

Nature of Christ's Return

First Coming

  • Came in human humility
  • Born in Bethlehem's manger
  • Seen by relatively few people

Second Coming

  • Returns as King of kings and Lord of lords (Revelation 19:16)
  • Comes with power and great glory (Matthew 24:30)
  • Will be seen by all humanity (Matthew 24:23-27)

The promise is repeatedly emphasized in Scripture (Acts 1:11; Philippians 3:20; 2 Timothy 4:8; Hebrews 11:8-13; 2 Peter 3:9,10,13; 1 John 2:28). It's unfortunate that many Christians ignore it. This decline in the church's first love for Christ's return has occurred partly due to the predicted weariness (Matthew 25:5) and partly due to the teaching that believers meet Jesus at the moment of death.

Additional Insights

Understanding the Rapture Question

Some believers teach that an invisible rapture of believers precedes Christ's glorious return. What is the basis for this belief, and why do Adventists not accept this interpretation?

Common Teaching (Example from Ryrie Study Bible on 1 Thessalonians 4:16,17):

"Then living believers will be 'raptured'... This rapture... of believers described here concerns both those who died and those alive when the Lord comes. His coming is here in the air, not on earth, and will occur just before the tribulation period (see Revelation 3:10). This period will conclude with His coming to earth (see Matthew 24:29-30; Revelation 19:11-16)."

Biblical Response:

  1. The Bible never speaks of a secret rapture
  2. 1 Thessalonians 4:16,17 does not describe anything secret
  3. The Bible never speaks of two returns of the Lord
  4. All biblical texts about Jesus returning to earth and saints going to heaven refer to one indistinguishable event
  5. The Bible always presents the saints being taken to heaven and the wicked being punished as simultaneous aspects of one great event
