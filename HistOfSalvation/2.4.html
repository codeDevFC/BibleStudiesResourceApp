Jesus Our Savior

Purpose:

To understand the importance and uniqueness of Jesus as our Savior.

Introduction

When we consider our lives and world conditions, we must honestly admit something is wrong. Life isn't what it should be. While we can rejoice in many good things, there is also injustice, suffering, struggle, illness, and death. We feel called to a better life but don't know how to achieve it. It's as if we've been dragged far from home and don't know how to return. The Bible describes this condition as being "lost" (Ezekiel 34:16; Matthew 15:24; 18:11; Luke 15:4,9,24; 19:10). This is why we need someone to bring us home.

The Bible calls this process "salvation" and the one who accomplishes it "Savior."

1) From what must we be saved?

Romans 6:23

We are sinners, people who have disobeyed the Father, left His house, and found themselves immersed in suffering and death, the ultimate consequence of sin. Salvation must fundamentally bring us out of this state of sin, which is alienation and death.

2) Why is Jesus our Savior?

1 Peter 2:24

Because He took our sin upon Himself, giving us forgiveness, and gave His life to reconcile us with God, bringing us back home.

3) Why is Jesus our only Savior?

Hebrews 2:14-18; 4:14-16

These texts tell us we were slaves to someone (Satan) and something (fear and sin). Through His act of love, Jesus:

  • Testifies to God's love
  • Defeats Satan
  • Enables forgiveness of our sins

Jesus can do all this because He Himself was not defeated by sin.

4) Why is Jesus our only mediator?

For at least two reasons:

1. His Nature

1 Timothy 2:5

He alone is both human and divine, and thus He alone can unite humanity with divinity.

2. His Holiness

Hebrews 4:14-16

He alone is without sin and can offer His life in our place.

5) How great was Jesus' sacrifice for us?

Philippians 2:5-8

Jesus is the Son of God, the Creator and King of the entire universe (Colossians 1:13-20).

This Supreme Being "made Himself nothing," becoming human and dying for us on the cross, the most shameful and infamous death humans had invented.

For Reflection: Considering what Jesus has done for us, how much and in what ways can we appreciate and love Him?

6) How should we respond to His love and humility?

Philippians 2:9-11

We must recognize Him as our Lord and should bow in humility and gratitude before Him. (We should not do this for any other living being, not even angels: Revelation 19:10; Acts 10:25,26.)

7) Is there anyone else besides Jesus who can save us?

Acts 4:12

No, He is the only one.

8) Can we earn salvation through our good works?

Romans 3:23,24

No, no one can save themselves. Salvation is a gift, it's by God's grace, and no one can pay for it by any means.

For Reflection: Can we think of paying for Jesus' sacrifice in any way? If we claimed to, wouldn't we devalue its meaning? What can we do instead?

9) Who can be saved? What must we do to be saved?

Everyone can be saved by:

  • Believing in Jesus (John 3:16,36)
  • Confessing with all our heart, that is, testifying to our faith before others (Romans 10:9-11)
