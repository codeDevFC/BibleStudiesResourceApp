Doctrine of Sanctification and Obedience

5.3 Law and Grace

Introduction

We know we are saved by grace. Jesus gave His life for this purpose, and we cannot repay Him in any way for what He has done. We must only receive His love and love Him in return with complete trust. By accepting Jesus, our sins are forgiven, and our relationship with the heavenly Father is fully restored. But what happens once we have accepted God's wonderful grace? Will we continue to disobey God, or will we learn to obey precisely because we love Him and trust in Him?

1. What is Grace?

Grace is a free act, an unmerited gift that someone offers in favor of another. God is the supreme exemplification of grace.

Hebrews 4:16

"Let us then approach God's throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need."

For Christians, everything is God's grace. Without Him, we would not even exist, we would have no awareness of our nature, no light, and no hope.

2. What Does the Bible Teach About Obtaining Salvation?

a. Man's Condition

Romans 3:23

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

Romans 6:23

"For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."

b. Human Limitation

Jeremiah 13:23

Man cannot become righteous by his own strength.

c. God's Solution

Romans 3:24

"And all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus."

Ephesians 2:8

"For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God."

3. Why Is It Important to Know We Are Saved by Grace Alone?

a. Eliminates Human Presumption

As Jesus taught, after doing all possible good, we must say: "We are unworthy servants; we have only done our duty" (Luke 17:7-10).

b. Makes Salvation Certain

Based not on our uncertain capabilities, but on the certainty of God's love.

Hebrews 6:19

"We have this hope as an anchor for the soul, firm and secure."

c. Offers Spiritual Peace

Romans 5:1

"Therefore, since we have been justified through faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ."

4. What is the Relationship Between Grace and Faith?

Faith is humanity's response to God's grace - our trust in His love and mercy. Salvation originates from God's initiative, who loved us and gave Jesus for us when we were sinners and enemies (Romans 5:10; 1 John 4:19), when we were far from Him and from all hope. Conquered by love, we learn to trust God and humbly begin to walk with Him again.

In the Bible, having faith always means having faith in someone - in God. When the Bible tells us that salvation is by grace, it invites us to look at God's love, the only foundation of hope. When it tells us salvation is by faith, it invites us to respond with trust and gratitude. Grace and faith are like two sides of the same coin.

Romans 4:16

"Therefore, the promise comes by faith, so that it may be by grace."

Illustration: Grace is God's hand offering, faith is man's hand reaching out to receive.

5. Does Being Saved by Grace Eliminate Our Need to Obey God's Law?

No! On the contrary, the gospel teaches that:

  • Jesus did not abolish the law but confirmed, extended, and purified it (Matthew 6:17-20)
  • We cannot live in sin using grace as an excuse (Romans 6:1,2; 1 John 3:4)
  • Christians are not just those who say they believe in God, but those who obey Him (Matthew 7:21)

6. What is the Best Reason for Obeying God's Commandments?

Love!

John 14:15

"If you love me, keep my commands."

We should serve God because God loved us first (1 John 4:19), not to earn merits or salvation itself.

Deep Dive: Common Questions

Historical Context

Current Protestantism emerged as a reaction to Catholic religiosity that emphasized human works for salvation. In rediscovering grace's value, they often took an antinomian position (against the law).

Paul's True Position

  • Paul persecuted Christ based on his human confidence in law-keeping
  • His salvation came through an unexpected revelation of Christ's grace
  • He preaches salvation by grace without works of law
  • He consistently affirms the law's value while opposing legalistic observance

James isn't contradicting Paul but addressing a misunderstanding of salvation by grace. While Paul often reacted against those relying on their works, James opposes those using grace as an excuse for disobedience. Both recognize that grace doesn't exclude works and that works should be the fruit of faith.

Colossians 2:13-14

"When you were dead in your sins and in the uncircumcision of your flesh, God made you alive with Christ. He forgave us all our sins, having canceled the charge of our legal indebtedness, which stood against us and condemned us; he has taken it away, nailing it to the cross."

This doesn't mean the law is abolished. The Greek term "cheirografon" refers to a certificate of debt, not the law itself. Christ cancelled our debt of sin, not God's moral law.

Let's Think Together

All Christians attribute great importance to the Ten Commandments, but only a few truly seek to honor them. Some have even changed their content. Others have simply set them aside. What will we do?

Do we love Jesus enough to trust Him and obey His commandments as He gave them to us?

Understanding Ephesians 2:14-16

Ephesians 2:14-16

"For he himself is our peace, who has made the two groups one and has destroyed the barrier, the dividing wall of hostility, by setting aside in his flesh the law with its commands and regulations. His purpose was to create in himself one new humanity out of the two, thus making peace, and in one body to reconcile both of them to God through the cross, by which he put to death their hostility."

Context and Meaning

  • The passage addresses Christ's salvation extending to Gentile believers
  • References the physical "wall of partition" in the temple that separated Gentiles
  • Christ abolished barriers between people and between humanity and God
  • This doesn't abolish God's moral law but removes it as a barrier of separation

Key Understanding

Paul doesn't identify the law itself as "the hostility." The law is "good," "righteous," and "holy" (Romans 7:12). Rather, our wrong approach to the law, through pride, creates hostility. Christ abolishes our pride, not the law (Romans 3:27).

Love and Law Fulfillment

Galatians 5:13-14

"You, my brothers and sisters, were called to be free. But do not use your freedom to indulge the flesh; rather, serve one another humbly in love. For the entire law is fulfilled in keeping this one command: 'Love your neighbor as yourself.'"

Love's Expression

The rest of the law serves to express what love is. Without concrete manifestation, love is merely sterile emotionalism.

Jesus's Teaching

As Jesus taught (Matthew 22:37-38), love is the foundation of the law, and only when the law is observed with love does it make us children of God who is love (1 John 4:8,16).

Practical Application

Love cannot be expressed without observing the law. How can one love their neighbor while violating commandments against murder, theft, or adultery?

Commandments vs. Counsel

Some try to distinguish between commandments (the Ten Commandments) and counsel, suggesting:

  • Commandments are important and obligatory
  • Counsel is less important and optional
  • Commandments are necessary while counsel is merely helpful

These distinctions are not biblical.

Equal Importance

The Ten Commandments are crucial, but within the law, the commandment to love God is equally important.

God's Authority

When God speaks, regardless of the words He uses, we cannot choose what to observe based on our wisdom.

Serious Matters

Deuteronomy 30:19; Revelation 3:18

When God gives counsel or presents choices, these always concern matters of life and death, not optional matters.

Final Reflection

There are evident differences within God's law, including how they are observed, but we must examine each aspect seeking to understand the value the Bible itself attributes to them, not based on predetermined criteria.

Key Points Summary

Grace

  • Unmerited divine favor
  • Foundation of salvation
  • Cannot be earned

Faith

  • Human response to grace
  • Trust in God's promises
  • Active, not passive

Law

  • Expression of God's character
  • Guide for Christian living
  • Fulfilled through love

  • How has God's grace manifested in your life?

  • How does grace change your relationship with God?

  • What role does trust play in your faith?
  • How does faith influence your daily decisions?
  • What strengthens your faith?

  • What is the difference between legalism and loving obedience?
  • How can you grow in loving obedience?
