5.8 Hygienic Laws: General Principles

10 min read
Introduction

Everyone knows the saying that "when there is health, there is everything." However, many suffer from illnesses and various health problems. Often this happens because of the unsuitable environment in which we live or because we have inherited a body already corrupted by sin. Sometimes, however, the fault lies in wrong habits. In any case, by acquiring healthier habits, we can protect our health and enjoy a better life. Would you like that? In the next three studies, we will discover what God has taught us about our health. Already now, it may be useful to know that Adventists enjoy on average a life that is 4-7 years longer and are less affected by certain diseases. If you want, this privilege can be yours too.

Let's discover what principles God has taught us to achieve this result.

1. The General Principle for Our Life
1 Corinthians 10:31

"So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God."

Since our entire life is a gift from God, this principle should guide all our choices, including those related to health.

2. God's Interest in Our Physical Well-being
3 John 2

"Dear friend, I pray that you may enjoy good health and that all may go well with you, even as your soul is getting along well."

3. Jesus' Concern for Physical Health
Matthew 4:24

"News about him spread all over Syria, and people brought to him all who were ill with various diseases, those suffering severe pain, the demon-possessed, those having seizures, and the paralyzed; and he healed them."

Jesus cared not only for spiritual well-being but also for physical healing, demonstrating God's concern for the whole person.

5. Benefits of Following God's Laws
Exodus 15:26

"He said, 'If you listen carefully to the LORD your God and do what is right in his eyes, if you pay attention to his commands and keep all his decrees, I will not bring on you any of the diseases I brought on the Egyptians, for I am the LORD, who heals you.'"

God promises health benefits to those who follow His instructions.

6. Ancient Health Principles

Quarantine

Leviticus 13:1-6

Prescribing quarantine for infectious diseases. It's remarkable to find such a law written in the Bible about 3,500 years ago, when people lacked knowledge about the value of quarantine.

Contamination Prevention

Leviticus 13:47-52

Prohibiting the use of potentially contaminated items.

Healthy Homes

Leviticus 14:34-45

Teaching about maintaining healthy homes. Shouldn't we also have clean homes where germs cannot spread? Water, light, and air are among the best natural tools to achieve this goal.

Handwashing

Leviticus 15:1-12

Requiring washing after touching potential sources of infection.

Food Safety

Deuteronomy 14:21

Teaching not to eat animals found dead.

Note: God doesn't force foreigners, who don't know His laws, to either eat or not eat these animals. But to His people, He offers something better.

Waste Management

Deuteronomy 23:12,14

Teaching care for organic waste. To appreciate such a simple law, consider that in civilized Europe a few centuries ago, people were still accustomed to throwing this waste on the street, even in cities like Florence.

7. Evaluating These Laws

When these laws were written (about 3,500 years ago), people knew nothing about viruses and microbes. They didn't know how epidemics spread. God couldn't use scientific language with them. That's why we find religious language. But we have the means to understand the hygienic value of these norms behind the religious motivations.

Sometimes, the language used to describe these diseases is so different from ours that we have some difficulty understanding its meaning. The general principles, however, are very clear: God cares for us and teaches us to take care of our hygiene to avoid any dangerous contact that could transmit diseases, or to wash ourselves if necessary.

How can we apply these principles to our lives?

8. Biblical Teaching on Rest
Mark 6:31

"Then, because so many people were coming and going that they did not even have a chance to eat, he said to them, 'Come with me by yourselves to a quiet place and get some rest.'"

Jesus took care of His disciples and often invited them to rest in a quiet place. Too often we live a stressful life. We need rest not only for our body but also for our mind and heart.

How can we relax in a Christian way?

9. Joy and Health
Proverbs 17:22

"A cheerful heart is good medicine, but a crushed spirit dries up the bones."

Modern psychosomatic medicine amply demonstrates the truth of this biblical saying. We now know how serenity and joy stimulate our body's natural defenses, helping us have better health.

10. Finding Rest in Jesus
Matthew 11:28

"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest."

Being happy isn't so easy. Many times we have so many problems that we can't be truly serene. Jesus wants to help us find true rest and peace.

Summary of Key Principles

God cares deeply about our physical health as well as our spiritual well-being. The Bible provides numerous health principles that were far ahead of their time:

  • Our bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit and should be treated with respect
  • What we eat and drink should glorify God
  • Biblical hygiene practices include quarantine, contamination prevention, and cleanliness
  • Proper rest is essential for good health
  • Mental health matters - joy and peace contribute to physical well-being
  • Jesus offers ultimate rest for our souls

By following these principles, we can experience better health and honor God with our bodies. As we continue in the next lesson, we'll explore more specific guidelines about food and nutrition.
