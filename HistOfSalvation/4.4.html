Doctine Of The Church

4.4 Confession

Purpose:

To understand the necessity and spiritual duty of confessing our sins, freeing it from the encrustations of auricular confession.

Introduction

We all carry burdens in our hearts that oppress us - remorse, awareness of sin, or wrongs committed. If we let everything remain in our hearts, it becomes like stagnant water that doesn't flow and becomes foul and harmful. God's word instead invites us to release this burden and free ourselves from it to restore a condition of serenity and peace, to remedy the wrong done whenever possible. The practice of confession responds to this need, but we must free it from misunderstandings and practices that have become associated with it over the centuries.

1. What Does "Confession" Mean?

It means becoming aware of something unjust we have done, of a wrong state of our heart, and recognizing it to receive forgiveness, help, and salvation.

2. Do We Have Examples of Confession in the Bible?

Daniel 9:4-19

The prophet Daniel confesses to God the sins of his people

Psalm 51:1-10

King David confesses to God his own sin

3. To Whom Should We Confess Our Sins?

  • Psalm 51:1; Daniel 9:4 - To whom did Daniel and David confess their sins?
  • Ezra 10:11 - Are these exceptions or do they reflect a general biblical teaching?

4. If Our Confession is Honest, What Must We Do About Our Sins?

Proverbs 28:13

We must not only confess but also forsake them.

5. If We Have Done Something Wrong Against Someone Else, What Must We Do?

Confession to Others

James 5:16; Matthew 18:15

We must confess what we have done to them as well

Making Amends

Numbers 5:7

We must make amends for the wrong done

6. Are We Certain That if We Confess Our Sins, God Will Forgive Us?

1 John 1:5-2:2

Yes, God promises complete forgiveness when we confess our sins.

7. Through Whom Can We Obtain God's Forgiveness?

1 John 1:7; Romans 3:23-24

Through Jesus Christ and His sacrifice for us.

8. Is There Any Sin That Cannot Be Forgiven?

Only the sin against the Holy Spirit (Matthew 12:31). Not because God doesn't want to forgive it, but because by refusing the Spirit's action, refusing the light He wants to give to our conscience (John 16:7,8), we become unaware of our sin and don't go to God for forgiveness.

9. Can Any Human Being Act as a Mediator Before God to Confess Our Sin and Obtain Forgiveness?

As we have seen, the Bible teaches that we must confess our sins to God, and only He has the power to forgive us (Mark 2:5-12). Only Jesus is our mediator (1 Timothy 2:5).

10. Who Should Confess Their Sin?

Romans 3:23

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

11. Should We Fear Going to God to Ask for Forgiveness?

Hebrews 4:14-16

How will He welcome us if we go to Him in Jesus' name?

John 20:23 Matthew 18:18

Jesus didn't give this power only to Peter or the Apostles but to the entire church (Matthew 18:15-19). Note that here, "church" refers not just to clergy but to the entire community of believers, to all who have received the Holy Spirit (John 20:21,22).

Important Understanding:

  • This authority is not to take God's place
  • It represents God's will in applying church discipline (Matthew 18:15-19)
  • It involves preaching the gospel of salvation in Jesus (John 20:21)
  • If people accept it, they will be saved; if they reject it, they will be condemned

Peter and the other apostles never taught that sinners should come to them for forgiveness, but only to God (Acts 8:20-22).
