5.6 The Sabbath in the New Testament

Introduction

The Sabbath was given at creation and has eternal value because this day celebrates God as our Father-Creator, and He will remain so forever. It is therefore natural that we find the Sabbath also in the New Testament.

1. Understanding Jesus' Perspective on the Law

Matthew 5:17-20

Some interpret these verses as if Jesus had fulfilled, finished the law, and therefore we no longer need to concern ourselves with it. If this were true, we would have to ask whether we have license to kill since Jesus perfectly fulfilled the commandment "do not kill."

Key Understanding:

  • Jesus said: "Think not that I am come to destroy... but to fulfill"
  • Greek word "pleroo" means "to fulfill, complete, bring to perfection"
  • Jesus revealed law's deep meaning of love and non-violence
  • Called for deeper observance than the Pharisees

2. Jesus' Sabbath Observance

Luke 4:15

Note: Jesus observed the Sabbath not as a Jewish custom but as His own habit.

3. Jesus' Teaching About Sabbath Observance

Luke 6:5

Lord of the Sabbath

Jesus is Lord of the Sabbath and can reveal its true meaning and how to observe it.

Matthew 12:12

Doing Good

"It is lawful to do good on the Sabbath." For Jesus, the Sabbath isn't inactivity but a day of love.

Mark 2:27-28

Made for Man

"The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the Sabbath."

  • Connects to creation purpose (Genesis 2:1-3)
  • Shows Sabbath as God's gift and blessing
  • Applies to all humanity, not just Israel

4. Did Jesus Teach Sabbath Abolition After His Death?

Matthew 24:20

Speaking of Jerusalem's destruction (70 AD), Jesus said to pray that flight wouldn't be in winter or on Sabbath.

Key Implications:

  • Assumes continued Sabbath observance after His death
  • Shows concern for Sabbath sanctity
  • Indicates ongoing validity for Christians

5. Early Christian Church Evidence

a) Terminology

In all NT writings, "Sabbath" maintains its meaning of "rest"

b) Paul's Practice

Acts 13:14,42,44; 16:13; 17:2

Regular Sabbath observance and teaching

c) Apocalyptic Vision

Revelation 14:6-13

References creation language from Sabbath commandment

6. Conclusion

Jesus, our Lord and model, observed the Sabbath and taught His disciples to do likewise, rediscovering its original meaning as God's gift, a sign of His love, and filling it with our love for God and others. This is what the early Christians did and what we want to continue doing.

Hebrews 4:9

"There remains, then, a Sabbath-rest (sabbatismos) for the people of God."

Three Types of Rest in Context:

  • Rest in Canaan after wilderness journey (vv. 1-3,5)
  • Seventh-day rest at creation (vv. 3,4)
  • Salvation rest (vv. 6-8)

Interpretation

All three aspects contribute to a complete understanding where God is the source of all rest:

  • Seventh-day Sabbath
  • Promised land rest
  • Peace in Christ
Galatians 4:10

"You observe days and months and seasons and years!"

Why This Doesn't Refer to Biblical Sabbath:

  • List doesn't match Old Testament festivals
  • Context refers to pagan practices (v. 8)
  • Addresses return to former idolatrous practices
  • Refers to astrological superstitions

Context Understanding

  • Not about weekly worship day
  • Connected to eating practices
  • Likely refers to fasting days
  • Addresses personal devotional practices

Practical Implications

  • Not about communal worship times
  • Preserves church unity
  • Addresses individual devotional practices
  • Maintains importance of corporate worship
Colossians 2:16,17

"Therefore do not let anyone judge you by what you eat or drink, or with regard to a religious festival, a New Moon celebration or a Sabbath day. These are a shadow of the things that were to come; the reality, however, is found in Christ."

Three Possible Interpretations:

  1. Freedom to observe or not observe
  2. Manner of observance
  3. Reasons for observance

Correct Understanding

Addresses manner of observance and unity in Christ, not abolition of Sabbath
