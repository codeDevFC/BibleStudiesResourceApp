When Will Jesus Return?

Purpose:

To understand that Jesus' return concerns our present life, not just a distant, indefinite time. Although we don't know the day or hour, we can understand that the signs of the times are being fulfilled and that we already live in the last days. In any case, whenever the Lord comes, we should live as if He were coming today. While cultivating the joy of His coming, we must avoid being seduced by presumed signs.

Jesus' return is so important that we wish it would happen immediately. If we had been with His disciples on the Mount of Olives, we too would have asked: "Tell us, when will these things be, and what will be the sign of Your coming and of the end of the age?" (Matthew 24:3). Jesus had just announced that of the temple they had admired, not one stone would be left upon another. It was a terrible announcement that had disturbed their minds. The temple was God's house; how could it be destroyed? Their minds probably went to "apocalyptic" scenarios of the world's end. So they asked Jesus about the time of the temple's destruction and the end of the present age, as if they were one and the same reality. Jesus, of course, knew these were different realities. We find His answer in Matthew 24 and Luke 21.

1) Can we know the moment when Jesus will return?

Matthew 24:1

No one knows the day (see also Acts 1:6,7)

2) Can we know something about the general period?

Matthew 24:32

"When you see these things, know that I am at the door."

3) Is there a danger of seeking false signs?

Matthew 24:4-11

Jesus warns about false signs and false messiahs that could deceive many.

4) What specific sign does Jesus offer to understand when the world's end is near?

Matthew 24:14

"This gospel of the kingdom will be preached in all the world as a witness to all nations, and then the end will come."

5) What specific sign does Jesus give regarding Jerusalem's temple?

Matthew 24:15-20

See explanation in Luke 21:20

6) What other signs does Jesus give?

Celestial Signs

Matthew 24:29
  • Signs in sun, moon, and stars
  • The "Dark Day" - May 19, 1780
  • Meteoric shower - November 13, 1833

These dates are significant in relation to prophetic times announcing the beginning of judgment in 1844 (Daniel 8:14).

Social and Moral Signs

  • Matthew 24:37-39 - Indifference to God
  • Matthew 24:12 - Moral corruption
  • Revelation 11:17,18 - Ecological destruction and modern warfare risks

7) Why has Jesus indicated signs but not the exact moment of His return?

Matthew 24:42-51
  • We must be ready every day
  • Not just when forced by fear of judgment
  • Understanding Jesus is near encourages preparation out of love

8) Do you desire to be ready to meet Jesus?

Matthew 7:21-27

"Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord'..."

For Reflection:

Being "Adventist," waiting for Christ's return, is more than waiting for Jesus at a chronologically determined time. This is important, but more important is living one's life in constant expectation of the Lord. He can return at any moment, and at any moment we desire to be ready.

Additional Insights

9. How Should We Evaluate Christians Seeing Signs in Their Time?

  1. Heart's Tendency

    The heart tends to see what it dreams. While this is a risk, it also testifies to love for Jesus' return. If we must choose, it's better to err through too much love than to be among those who don't err because of disinterest.

  2. Growing Prophetic Understanding

    The Bible itself predicts that knowledge of prophetic messages will increase toward the end times (Daniel 12:4). Without this better understanding, misinterpretation was possible.

  3. Better Hermeneutical Understanding

    Better understanding of interpretative principles now helps distinguish true signs from false ones. For example, wars and earthquakes mentioned in Matthew 24 are often mistakenly considered as signs, when Jesus actually warns against this interpretation.

  4. Indeterminate Signs

    Some "signs" like moral corruption or lack of faith are inherently indeterminate. Faith is never enough, and immorality is always too much in this world.

10. Does Waiting for Heaven's Kingdom Alienate Us from Earthly Engagement?

Christian hope should actually increase engagement for a better present:

Romans 14:17

"For the kingdom of God is not eating and drinking, but righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Spirit."

Fruits of the Spirit

Galatians 5:22

"Love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control"

Two Key Principles:

  1. Kingdom hope leads to present kingdom values
  2. Future certainty encourages present perseverance

13. Understanding Specific Time References

The Mission of the Twelve (Matthew 10:23)

Context:

Jesus tells the disciples that the "Son of Man" would come before they finished preaching the gospel.

Important Considerations:
  • Limited Geographic Scope:
    • "Do not go among the Gentiles" (Matthew 10:5-6)
    • Only to "the lost sheep of Israel"
    • Temporary, local mission
  • Immediate Context:
    • Short-term separation from Jesus
    • Mission lasted days or weeks
    • Jesus continued teaching in their cities (Matthew 11:1)

The Transfiguration Promise

Matthew 16:28; Mark 9:1; Luke 9:27
Understanding the Promise:
  • Not referring to final kingdom manifestation
  • Refers to Transfiguration preview
  • Three disciples witnessed Christ's glory
  • Represents two groups of saved:
    • Moses - Those who die and are resurrected
    • Elijah - Those translated without seeing death
Textual Support:
  • All three gospels follow this saying with Transfiguration account
  • Specific time references in each account
  • Direct fulfillment in Peter, James, and John's experience

Key Takeaways

  • We cannot know the exact time of Jesus' return
  • We can recognize the signs of His coming
  • We should live in constant readiness
  • Our preparation should be motivated by love, not fear
