Doctrine Of The Church

The Christian Church

Purpose:

To emphasize the communal value of Christian faith as a sign of restored creation harmony and as an anticipation of God's Kingdom peace. To help understand that Christ invites us to join a community of faith, service, and hope.

Introduction

No one can live alone. We exist as human beings because we were born from other human beings and grew up with them. From others, we learned to speak, to take care of ourselves, to behave as worthy men and women. Most of what we have comes from others. Try to remove from your life what you haven't personally discovered and done, and you'll see that not much remains.

1. What Does "Church" Mean?

Origin of the Word

The word "church" comes from the Greek "ecclesia" (meaning "called out"), referring to a community called together for a specific purpose. In a general sense, even a sports association or political party is an "ecclesia."

Every Church Has:

  • Members
  • Structure
  • Regulations
  • Purpose

Christian Church Distinctives

The Christian church differs primarily in:

  • Who establishes the ecclesia
  • Its purposes
  • How these purposes are achieved

2. Did Jesus Intend to Create His Own Church?

Matthew 16:18

"And I tell you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades will not overcome it."

Key Points:

  • Jesus explicitly expressed His intention to build His church
  • He assured that no one could defeat this project
  • It would be founded on a "solid rock"

We will consider Peter's role later. For now, let's focus on the main fact: Jesus wanted to build His own particular church.

3. What Does the New Testament Compare the Church To?

A People and Family

Ephesians 2:19

A people including all nations, races, social groups, a family where all are God's children loving one another.

See also: Colossians 3:11, John 13:35

A Living Temple

Ephesians 2:20-22

A living temple where:

  • Believers are the stones
  • Apostles and prophets are the foundation
  • Jesus is the cornerstone

A Body

1 Corinthians 12:12-27

A body with Christ as the head, where members support each other.

A Faithful Bride

2 Corinthians 11:1,2

A faithful bride remaining pure for her bridegroom, Jesus.

See also: Ephesians 5:25-27

4. How Large Should We Expect the Christian Church to Be?

Luke 12:32

"Do not be afraid, little flock, for your Father has been pleased to give you the kingdom."

The Gospel Commission

  • Matthew 24:14 - Worldwide preaching
  • Matthew 28:19 - Making disciples
  • Mark 16:15 - Reaching all creation

Reality of Response

Jesus taught that only a few would accept the message Matthew 22:14

Future Glory

In God's kingdom, the saved will be an innumerable multitude Revelation 7:9

Consider Elijah's example: Though he felt alone, God had preserved 7,000 faithful believers 1 Kings 19:14,18

5. Is Fellowship with Other Believers Important?

Hebrews 10:25

"Let us not give up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but let us encourage one another—and all the more as you see the Day approaching."

1. Mutual Encouragement

Together we become stronger against Satan and life's difficulties. Like penguins in Antarctica, we survive by staying close to the group, especially in harsh conditions.

2. Living Testimony

Our unity demonstrates our Christian identity John 13:35. Church attendance isn't a ritual but a living experience of love for God and His children.

3. Church Growth

Through this fellowship, the Lord adds new believers to the church Acts 2:46,47

6. What Does Revelation Teach About the End-Time Church?

Revelation 14:12

"Here is the patience of the saints; here are those who keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus."

Key Characteristics:

  • Observes God's commandments
  • Maintains faith in Jesus
  • Demonstrates perseverance

7. What Special Appeal Does Revelation Make?

Revelation 18:1-4

"Come out of her, my people..."

The Babylon Symbol

Historical Context:

  • Babylon was a religious and political power opposing God's people
  • Israel was held captive in Babylon
  • God sent Cyrus to destroy Babylon and free His people

Modern Significance:

  • A "Babylon" still exists, trying to control and oppose the Christian church
  • God calls His people to leave Babylon and join His true people
  • This happens when we leave religious errors and join God's people
  • Complete fulfillment comes at Christ's return when "Babylon" is destroyed

Key References:

Isaiah 44:28 Isaiah 45:1 2 Chronicles 36:22,23 John 14:1-3 Philippians 3:20 Hebrews 13:14
