God is Innocent of Our Sufferings (Sin)

Purpose:

To explain the meaning of sin and understand how evil could emerge from a perfect creation, preparing us for understanding salvation and our trust in Jesus.

Introduction

Too much suffering, too much disorder, too much injustice. How often are we tempted to say: "Why, if God exists, does He allow all this?" But before blaming God, we should ask ourselves: "Why is all this happening? Are we sure it's God's responsibility?"

1) What kind of world did God create?

Genesis 1:31

The Bible states that God created everything good. There was harmony, peace, and abundant life for all His creatures. This peace was so complete that no one was allowed to eat any living being except plants.

Genesis 1:29,30

2) Could man eat from the tree of life?

Genesis 2:16,17

Yes! The tree of life represents two fundamental truths:

  1. It represents life itself, teaching us that our life comes from God, whose desire is for us to live. The tree was there for its fruit to be freely eaten.
  2. It teaches us that life is not something we naturally possess (we are not immortal by nature): the tree of life is outside, not inside us.
Revelation 22:1,2

This tree, in God's future kingdom, has its roots in the river of life that flows from the throne of God and of the Lamb (Jesus): life comes from God.

3) Which tree was forbidden and why?

Genesis 2:17

The tree of knowledge of good and evil represented something God wanted to reserve for Himself. Eating from it meant:

  • Disobeying God
  • Not trusting Him
  • Claiming a right that wasn't ours
  • Ultimately, committing sin

In biblical times, "knowledge" meant establishing a relationship of unity, not just theoretical understanding.

Genesis 4:1 - For man and woman, to know each other means to become one

Jeremiah 22:15,16; 24:7 - To know God means to love Him and do His will

4) How does the story continue with the serpent's temptation?

The serpent in ancient world symbolized:

  • Source of life (annual renewal through shedding skin)
  • Source of fear and death (venom and deceptive approach)

Revelation 12:9 reveals that behind the serpent was Satan himself.

5) What was the nature of the temptation?

Genesis 3:1-5
  1. Questioning God's love and character
  2. Suggesting human autonomy regarding life
  3. Promoting independence in moral decisions

6) What were the consequences of sin?

Genesis 3:7

Inner harmony broken

Genesis 3:9-10

Relationship with God broken

Genesis 3:12

Man-woman harmony broken

Genesis 3:16

Violence enters human relationships

Genesis 3:17,18

Harmony with nature broken

Genesis 3:19

Death enters the world

8) How does John summarize the nature of sin?

1 John 3:4

"Everyone who sins breaks the law; in fact, sin is lawlessness."

9) How does Paul summarize this study's argument?

Romans 6:23

"For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."

10) Why did God allow evil to arise and manifest?

1 John 4:8; Galatians 5:13,14

These texts teach two interconnected truths:

  • God is love
  • He calls us to freedom

Love requires freedom of choice - it cannot be forced. God created us with the freedom to choose between accepting or rejecting Him.

For Reflection:

  1. Knowing sin's tragic consequences, what position should we take regarding God's law?
  2. How should we evaluate God's attitude who, though offended and rejected, continues to love us and work for our salvation?
  3. Can we also be tempted to blame God for suffering? How can we overcome this?
