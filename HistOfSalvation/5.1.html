Doctrine of Sanctification and Obedience

5.1 Prayer

Purpose:

To understand and experience prayer as humble dialogue with God to praise Him and receive light and help.

Introduction

Ellen G. White defined prayer as "the breath of the soul." Just as we cannot live physically without breathing, spiritually, we cannot live without praying.

1. What is Prayer?

Philippians 4:6

A dialogue with God in which we open our hearts to Him and seek His voice.

Prayer Should Include:

1. Praise

Psalm 100:4; Revelation 19:5

2. Gratitude

Colossians 4:2

3. Intercession

Colossians 4:3; Ephesians 6:18,19

4. Listening

Prayer cannot be only speaking without listening. Listening occurs when we allow God's answer to work in our lives with our cooperation, when the Spirit's voice finds a sensitive heart.

Meditation on God's Word can be considered part of the prayer experience.

2. To Whom Should Prayers Be Directed?

Philippians 4:6; Matthew 6:9

Regarding Catholicism

With its array of mediators, we affirm that prayer directed straight to God is the inalienable privilege the heavenly Father gives to all His children.

Regarding Secularism

Entering Christianity, we affirm that prayer is not just internal psychological examination but a real relationship with Another outside ourselves, not to be confused with seeking one's own self common in Eastern ascetic practices.

3. What Example of Prayer Did Jesus Give Us?

Matthew 6:9-14

This is not meant to be memorized, although that may be desirable. It's never repeated in the New Testament church experience. Rather, it's an example of prayer, characterized by:

  • Simplicity
  • Direct dialogue with the Father
  • Trust
  • Selflessness

We can certainly use it in community liturgical moments or as personal prayer, but if we limited ourselves to memorized recitation, we would do the opposite of what Jesus wants to teach us.

Why not try commenting on this prayer together?

4. Many Fear Not Knowing How to Pray. What Consolation Does the Gospel Give Us?

Matthew 6:7,8

We Can Learn

What does this teach us about mechanically repeated prayers?

Romans 8:26

The Spirit Helps Us

The Spirit sustains us and intercedes for us.

5. Is It Possible to Pray for One Another?

Matthew 5:44 Ephesians 6:18 Colossians 4:3

Understanding Intercessory Prayer

  • Praying for others shows love and responsibility
  • Those who pray for others aren't necessarily better or more worthy
  • They appeal to God's perfect love
  • We all have the duty to pray for others
  • We all need others to pray for us
  • It's a humble and reciprocal experience

6. Can We Pray to Saints to Pray to God on Our Behalf?

No!

As we've seen, our prayers should be directed straight to God. Throughout the Bible, we never find a prayer directed to anyone but Him. This isn't by chance, but because this is God's teaching.

7. But if Paul Asks the Church to Pray for Him, Why Can't We Ask Saints to Pray for Us? Aren't They Also Part of the Church?

The State of the Dead

Yes, they are part of the church. But the people considered Saints are dead, and the dead know nothing (Ecclesiastes 9:5. See lesson on the state of the dead).

Misconception of Saints' Role

  • Their help is invoked because they're thought to be holier than others
  • They're believed to have special merits before God
  • These merits supposedly help gain God's favor for us

In this perspective, we are outside God's grace (Romans 3:24) and don't honor Him.

Biblical Understanding

In the gospel, we're invited to pray for each other not because some are better than others, but simply because we're God's family and prayer expresses mutual love, our being family before the common Father.

8. In Whose Name Should Prayers Be Directed to God?

John 14:13,14

In Jesus' name

Praying in Jesus' Name Means:

1. Recognizing our unworthiness before God

2. Appealing to God through the same love He expressed for us through Jesus

This isn't about considering Jesus more benevolent than the Father but recognizing Him as the expression of God's love (John 14:7,9; 15:9).

9. What Did Jesus Teach About Mediators in Prayer?

John 16:26,27

We pray in Jesus' name, our only mediator (1 Timothy 2:5).

Jesus as Advocate

Jesus is our "advocate" with God (1 John 2:1,2). Yet, Jesus says He doesn't need to convince the Father to be benevolent toward us "because the Father himself loves you."

Purpose of the Advocate Image

This image exists not to suggest God needs convincing, but to convince us to trust we're welcomed by God despite our unworthiness.

Key Remember

Jesus came to manifest God's already existing love (John 3:16), not to convince God to love us.

10. With What Attitude Should We Come Before God?

Hebrews 4:14-16
  • God sent His Son to save us
  • Jesus is the creator of the universe (John 1:1-3)
  • Yet He became like us, humbling Himself to die on the cross (Philippians 2:5-11)
  • He did this to convince us of God's deep love
  • If we accept Jesus as our Savior, Mediator, and Friend, we have everything (Romans 8:17,32)

Practical Prayer Guide

Getting Started

Set a Regular Time

Choose a specific time each day for prayer, making it a consistent habit.

Find a Quiet Place

Select a peaceful location where you can focus without distractions.

Keep a Prayer Journal

Write down prayer requests, answers, and spiritual insights.

Prayer Structure

Adoration

Begin by praising God for who He is

Confession

Honestly share your struggles and sins

Thanksgiving

Express gratitude for God's blessings

Supplication

Present your requests and intercessions

Overcoming Common Prayer Challenges

Distractions

Write down distracting thoughts to address later, helping maintain focus during prayer.

Feeling Unworthy

Remember that access to God is through Jesus' merit, not our own.

Limited Time

Start with short, regular prayers rather than irregular long ones.

Key Takeaways

Direct Access

We can approach God directly through Jesus Christ, our only mediator.

Two-Way Communication

Prayer involves both speaking and listening to God.

Community Aspect

We're called to pray both individually and for one another.

God's Love

Prayer is based on God's existing love, not our worthiness.
