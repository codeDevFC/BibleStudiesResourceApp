Doctrine of Man

Purgatory

Purpose:

To demonstrate how the idea of purgatory is not only unbiblical but anti-biblical. It conflicts with biblical teaching about the nature of death and grace as the only way of salvation. To help instead trust in God's grace through Jesus Christ.

The Bible completely ignores any place equivalent to what is known as purgatory, where the dead expiate their sins' punishment or are purified from their carnal tendencies before being admitted to God's holy presence. In fact, it implicitly but clearly excludes it. While the Bible teaches that we are contaminated by sin and need purification, the way this happens is totally different from what purgatory commonly means.

1) Does the Bible use the word "purgatory"?

The Bible uses many terms related to the need or reality of purification, but never in relation to a place where one goes after death.

2) If purgatory doesn't exist, how can we be purified from our sins?

"But if we walk in the light as He is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus Christ His Son cleanses us from all sin." (1 John 1:7)

Purgatory is not a place but a Person: Jesus, God's Son who gave His life to free us from our sins.

Additional References:

  • Psalm 51:1-7 - God purifies us by His grace
  • Isaiah 53:11-12 - The Messiah bears our sins and clothes us with righteousness
  • Acts 15:8,9 - God purifies our hearts through faith
  • Hebrews 10:19-22 - Jesus' blood purifies us
  • 1 John 1:9 - If we confess our sins, Jesus forgives and purifies us

3) Why does the doctrine of purgatory contradict the Gospel?

Nature of Death

It assumes conscious existence of the disembodied soul after death, denying biblical teaching about human nature and the state of the dead (Ecclesiastes 9:10)

Salvation by Grace

It assumes purification through personal suffering, denying biblical teaching about salvation by grace through faith in Jesus (Romans 3:23,24; 5:1,2)

Immediate Purification

It assumes a long purification process, denying biblical doctrine about Jesus' grace purifying us here and now (1 John 1:7)

Personal Responsibility

It assumes others can shorten one's time in purgatory, denying biblical teaching about personal responsibility for salvation (Ezekiel 18:20)

4) What have been the consequences of purgatory doctrine in Christian church history?

Commercialization of Faith

It turned Christian faith into commerce, encouraging people to pay the church for release from purgatory (1 Peter 1:18-19; Matthew 10:8; Acts 8:20)

Conscience Control

The church used claimed power over purgatory to dominate people's consciences

Christian Division

It was a major cause of Christianity's division during the Protestant Reformation
