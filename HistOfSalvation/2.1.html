God as the Powerful and Loving Creator

Purpose:

To highlight the importance of the doctrine of creation in our time and to clarify the philosophical rather than scientific nature of the theory of evolution.

Introduction

All Christians believe that God created "the heavens and the earth," as stated in Genesis 1:1. However, not everyone believes that God created a good and peaceful world. Many believe that God created life through evolution. In this perspective, since evolution unfolds through violence and death, it logically follows that God becomes responsible for all the evil in the world.

Can we accept such an understanding?

Can we reconcile the Bible with what evolutionism claims?

1) According to the Bible, where does everything originate?

Genesis 1:1

God made all things.

2) After creating the earth, did God immediately create life upon it?

Genesis 1:2

The first verses of the Bible can be understood in different ways. The best interpretation seems to be that the "beginning" mentioned in verse 1 should be distinguished from the subsequent account of creation. In this perspective, after creating the universe and the planet Earth, the latter remained for an unspecified period "formless and empty," without life. This period could approximately correspond to the age attributed to the universe by astronomers.

3) How long did God take to prepare our planet for life and to be our home?

In six days, the earth was ready to welcome humanity, which was created on the sixth day.

See also Exodus 20:11

4) What kind of world did God create?

Genesis 1:4

Everything created by God was good, complete, and perfect, needing no improvement.

See also: Genesis 1:10, 12, 18, 21, 25

  • Each part of creation was "good"
  • The whole was "very good" (Genesis 1:31)
  • Everything was in harmony with God and creation
  • All beings were vegetarian (Genesis 1:29-30)

It was a truly peaceful and extraordinary world, the exact opposite of what evolutionism assumes.

5) How does the Bible describe God's method of creation?

Genesis 1:1

Ancient Peoples' View:

Creation through struggle, war, and death

Biblical View:

God creates effortlessly through His word

Psalm 33:9

"For He spoke, and it came to be; He commanded, and it stood firm"

6) If God created everything good, why do we see evil and corruption everywhere?

Romans 8:20-23

The Corruption of Perfect Creation:

  • Genesis 3 - Sin brought universal corruption
  • Genesis 3:17-19 - Death came as consequence
  • Romans 6:23 - Sin's ultimate result

Evolutionism Claims:

Violence and death are part of God's creation

Bible Teaches:

Evil originates from sin and rebellion against God

God is a loving Creator; humanity has destroyed what He gave us.

7) What significance does all this have for the Christian faith?

The fundamental issue goes beyond the temporal conflict between views:

Bible Theistic Evolution
God creates with absolute power, instantaneously and effortlessly. God creates while subject to natural laws, over a long period, with struggle.
God creates a world immediately perfect, aligned with His will. God creates through trial and error, with suffering.
God's ability to create perfectly gives hope for the future. Uncertainty about future perfection if God hasn't achieved it yet.
Sin corrupted creation; God isn't responsible for evil. God is responsible for natural evil; sin is irrelevant.
Jesus saves from sin, recreating all things (Revelation 21:1) Jesus continues God's struggle to perfect creation.

Reflection:

Today, our entire culture is influenced by evolutionism. How can we uphold our faith in the Good Creator God?
